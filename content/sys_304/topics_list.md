Based on the syllabus and supporting course materials, here is a comprehensive list of high-level topics you should focus on for review. These are formatted as searchable terms to help you find tutorials and lectures online.

### **1. Decision Making Under Uncertainty & Risk**
*   **Expected Monetary Value (EMV):** How to calculate the weighted average of outcomes using probabilities.
*   **Laplace Criterion:** Decision making based on the "principle of insufficient reason" (equal probability).
*   **Maximin and Maximax Criteria:** Pessimistic (best of the worst) vs. optimistic (best of the best) decision rules.
*   **Hurwicz Criterion:** Using an "index of optimism" ($\alpha$) to balance best and worst outcomes.
*   **Minimax Regret Criterion:** Understanding opportunity loss and minimizing maximum regret.

### **2. Bayesian Decision Theory & Experimentation**
*   **Bayes’ Rule / Bayes’ Theorem:** Calculating **posterior probabilities** based on new experimental information.
*   **Expected Value of Perfect Information (EVPI):** Determining the upper bound value of removing all uncertainty.
*   **Expected Value of Sample Information (EVSI) / Experimentation (EVE):** Assessing if a test or survey is worth its cost.

### **3. Utility Theory & Risk Attitudes**
*   **Subjective Utility Theory:** Transforming monetary values into "utiles" based on personal preference.
*   **Risk Aversion, Risk Seeking, and Risk Neutrality:** Understanding how the shape of a **utility curve** reflects risk attitudes.
*   **Equivalent Lottery Method:** A technique for constructing a utility curve by finding points of indifference.

### **4. Multicriteria Decision Making (MCDM)**
*   **Trade Studies in Systems Engineering:** Formal processes for selecting the best architectural or design alternatives.
*   **Analytic Hierarchy Process (AHP):** A structured technique for organizing and analyzing complex decisions using pairwise comparisons.
*   **Pugh Matrix / Decision Matrix:** Using criteria weighting and rating to compare alternatives.
*   **Additive Weighting Method:** Tabular and graphical approaches to scoring alternatives against multiple independent criteria.

### **5. Systems Analysis & Goal Development**
*   **Six Phases of Systems Analysis:** From determining goals to the "action" phase.
*   **Top-Down vs. Bottom-Up Planning:** Goal-centered visionary planning versus status-quo incrementalism.
*   **Objectives Tree / Goals Tree:** Hierarchical mapping of system objectives from general to specific.
*   **Descriptive vs. Normative Scenarios:** Documenting "as-is" conditions versus "to-be" ideal states.

### **6. Economic Evaluation & Optimization**
*   **Net Present Value (NPV) / Present Net Worth (PNW):** Discounting future cash flows to current value.
*   **Internal Rate of Return (IRR):** Finding the interest rate that makes NPV zero and the "fallacy" of using it for rankings.
*   **Life-Cycle Costing (LCCA):** Estimating the total cost of ownership from acquisition to disposal.
*   **Linear Programming (Simplex Method):** Mathematical optimization of a linear objective function subject to constraints.

### **7. Project Management & Control Tools**
*   **PERT / CPM (Critical Path Method):** Network planning for projects including slack time and deterministic/probabilistic scheduling.
*   **Gantt Charts:** Visualizing project schedules, milestones, and resource allocation.
*   **DELTA Charts:** Integrating Decisions, Events, Logic, Time, and Activity into a flowchart.

### **8. Reliability & Dependability Analysis**
*   **MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures):** The primary measure of system reliability.
*   **FMECA (Failure Mode, Effects, and Criticality Analysis):** Systematically identifying how a system can fail and the impact of those failures.
*   **Fault Tree Analysis (FTA):** A top-down, deductive graphical enumeration of failure causes.
*   **Quality Function Deployment (QFD) / House of Quality:** Translating customer needs into technical requirements.
