### Question 2: Independent Criteria in Trade Studies

Lecture Reference: The concept of independent criteria is mentioned in "01 Trade Studies and Decision Analysis in Systems Engineering (1).pdf" on page 42.

[6 points] Three evaluation criteria that should be independent of each other:

When selecting between two software vendors, the criteria should measure distinct aspects of the vendor's offering so that the performance in one area does not automatically determine performance in another. Here are three independent criteria:

- Total Cost of Ownership (TCO): This includes the initial license fees, implementation costs, and ongoing maintenance and support fees over a defined period (e.g., 5 years). This criterion is largely independent of the software's functionality or the vendor's stability.
- Functionality Fit: The percentage of the firm's key functional requirements that are met by the software "out-of-the-box" or through minimal customization. A vendor could offer a product with excellent functionality at a high cost, or a less functional product at a low cost.
- Vendor Stability and Support Quality: This measures the vendor's financial health, market reputation, and the quality of their technical support and user community. A large, stable vendor might have higher costs but better support, while a smaller, innovative vendor might have excellent functionality but less certain long-term stability.

These three criteria are independent because a high score in one (e.g., low cost) does not guarantee a high score in another (e.g., functionality or vendor stability).

[4 points] Explain why redundancy in criteria should be avoided:

Redundancy in criteria should be avoided for two main reasons, as implied by the lecture's direction to keep the number of criteria to a minimum and ensure they are independent:

- Skewed Weighting (Double-Counting): If two criteria are essentially measuring the same underlying attribute, that attribute is effectively given more weight in the final decision than intended. For example, if a trade study includes both "Cost" and "Profitability," they are highly correlated.
