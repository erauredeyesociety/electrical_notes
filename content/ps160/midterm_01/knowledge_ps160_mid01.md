# PS 160 — Midterm 1 Knowledge Questions (with answers)

## Fluids

**1. [2 points]** Write the definitions of density and pressure.

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V} \qquad p = \frac{F_\perp}{A}$$

**2. [2 points]** Write the hydrostatic condition and its solution for constant density.

$$\frac{dp}{dy} = -\rho g \qquad p(y) = p_0 - \rho g y$$

**3. [2 points]** Write the continuity equation and Bernoulli's equation.

$$\rho A v = C \qquad p + \rho g y + \tfrac{1}{2}\rho v^2 = C_0$$

*Additional knowledge:* gauge pressure, Pascal's law, volume flow rate $dV/dt = Av$, $\rho_\text{water} = 1000\ \text{kg/m}^3$, $p_\text{atm} = 101{,}325\ \text{Pa}$.

## Oscillations

**4. [2 points]** Write the differential equation for simple harmonic motion and its solution.

$$\frac{d^2 x}{dt^2} + \omega^2 x = 0 \qquad x(t) = A\cos(\omega t + \varphi)$$

**5. [3 points]** Write the formulas for the angular frequency of small oscillations of a mass-spring system, a simple pendulum, and a physical pendulum.

$$\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}} \qquad \omega = \sqrt{\frac{g}{\ell}} \qquad \omega = \sqrt{\frac{mgd}{I}}$$

**6. [3 points]** Write the relations between the frequency, angular frequency, and period.

$$f = \frac{1}{T} = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} \qquad \omega = 2\pi f = \frac{2\pi}{T} \qquad T = \frac{1}{f} = \frac{2\pi}{\omega}$$

*Additional knowledge:* Hooke's law, velocity $\vec v = d\vec r/dt$, acceleration $\vec a = d\vec v/dt$, kinetic energy $K = mv^2/2$, elastic potential energy $U = kx^2/2$.

## Waves and Sound

**7. [3 points]** Write the wave function for a right-going wave, and the definitions of wavenumber and wave speed.

$$y(x,t) = A\cos(kx - \omega t) \qquad k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \qquad v = \lambda f = \frac{\omega}{k}$$

**8. [3 points]** Write the formulas for the speed of waves on a string, in a solid, and in a fluid.

$$v = \sqrt{\frac{F}{\mu}} \qquad v = \sqrt{\frac{Y}{\rho}} \qquad v = \sqrt{\frac{B}{\rho}}$$

**9. [2 points]** Write the formulas for the frequencies and wavelengths of standing waves.

$$f_n = \frac{nv}{2L} \text{ or } \frac{nv}{4L} \qquad \lambda_n = \frac{2L}{n} \text{ or } \frac{4L}{n}$$

**10. [2 points]** Write the formulas for the average power of waves on a string and the intensity of sound waves.

$$P_\text{av} = \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{\mu F}\,\omega^2 A^2 \qquad I = \tfrac{1}{2}\sqrt{\rho B}\,\omega^2 A^2 = \frac{p_\text{max}^2}{2\sqrt{\rho B}}$$

**11. [2 points]** Write the formulas for the pressure amplitude of sound waves and the intensity-distance relationship.

$$p_\text{max} = B k A \qquad I = \frac{P}{4\pi r^2}$$

**12. [2 points]** Write the formulas for the sound intensity level and the reference intensity.

$$\beta = (10\ \text{dB})\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{I}{I_0}\right) \qquad I_0 = 10^{-12}\ \text{W/m}^2$$

**13. [2 points]** Write the formulas for the Doppler shifted frequency (source to the right of listener) and the beat frequency.

$$f_L = f_S \frac{v + v_L}{v + v_S} \qquad f_\text{beat} = |f_a - f_b|$$

*Additional knowledge:* superposition principle, wave equation $\dfrac{\partial^2 y}{\partial t^2} = v^2 \dfrac{\partial^2 y}{\partial x^2}$, $v_\text{sound} = 343\ \text{m/s}$.
