# PS 160 — Midterm 2 Knowledge Questions

Thermodynamics knowledge-question bank for Midterm 2 (Modules 17, 18, 19, 20).

These are fill-in-the-formula style questions — know each answer by heart.

---

## Knowledge questions

**1. [3 pts] Thermal expansion.**
$$\Delta L = \alpha L_0\,\Delta T,\quad \Delta V = \beta V_0\,\Delta T,\quad \beta = 3\alpha$$

**2. [2 pts] Heat capacity and latent heat.**
$$Q = m c\,\Delta T = n C\,\Delta T,\quad Q = \pm m L$$

**3. [4 pts] Molar heat capacities at constant volume and pressure.**
$$C_V = \frac{f}{2} R,\quad C_P = C_V + R,\quad \gamma = \frac{C_P}{C_V},\quad C_V = 3 R\text{ (solid)}$$

**4. [2 pts] Energy transfer rate — conduction and blackbody radiation.**
$$H = k A\,\frac{\Delta T}{L},\quad H = A\sigma T^4$$

**5. [3 pts] Ideal gas law.**
$$pV = NkT,\quad pV = nRT,\quad k N_A = R$$

**6. [3 pts] Equipartition theorem and Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution.**
$$\left\langle\tfrac{1}{2}m v^2\right\rangle = \tfrac{3}{2} k T,\quad v_\text{rms} = \sqrt{\langle v^2\rangle},\quad f(v) \propto v^2 e^{-mv^2/(2kT)}$$

**7. [4 pts] Zeroth, first, and second laws.**
$$T_A = T_B = T_C,\quad dU = \bar d Q - \bar d W,\quad W = Q = Q_H + Q_C\text{ (cycle)},\quad \Delta S\ge 0$$

**8. [3 pts] Work and entropy definitions.**
$$W = \int p\,dV,\quad \Delta S = \int\frac{\bar d Q}{T},\quad S = k\ln w$$

**9. [3 pts] Engine efficiency and COP (benefit/cost).**
$$e = \frac{W}{Q_H},\quad K_\text{refrig} = \frac{Q_C}{-W},\quad K_\text{heat pump} = \frac{-Q_H}{-W}$$

Equivalently, taking all quantities positive:
$$e = 1 - |Q_C|/Q_H,\quad K_\text{refrig} = Q_C/|W|,\quad K_\text{HP} = |Q_H|/|W|$$

**10. [1 pt] Entropy change over a Carnot cycle.**
$$\Delta S_\text{Carnot} = \frac{Q_H}{T_H} + \frac{Q_C}{T_C} = 0$$

## Additional things to know

- **Thermal stress:** $F/A = -Y\alpha\Delta T$.
- **Temperature scales:** K, R (Rankine), °C, °F.
- **Direction of heat flow** for engines (hot → work + cold) and refrigerators (cold → work → hot).
- **Basic processes on ideal gas** (adiabatic, isobaric, isochoric, isothermal) — know $Q$, $W$, $\Delta U$ formulas.
- **Engine efficiency formulas** including Carnot: $e_\text{Carnot} = 1 - T_C/T_H$.
