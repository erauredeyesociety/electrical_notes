Same problem set as M17 Review (no solutions provided in worksheet). See `M17_Review.md` for full worked solutions.

## Problems

1. Given $T = 201$ K, convert to degrees Fahrenheit.

2. A metal has $\alpha = 2.6 \times 10^{-5}$ (C°)$^{-1}$. A pipe 1.7 m long has its length increase by 7.4 mm. Find the temperature increase.

3. Metal with $\alpha = 1.1 \times 10^{-5}$ (C°)$^{-1}$, pipe $L_0 = 5.2$ m warmed by 46 C°. Find $\Delta L$ in mm.

4. Glass bottle $V_0 = 0.012$ m$^3$ filled to the brim with fluid. Warmed by 7.1 C°, 16 mL spills. Glass $\alpha = 1.9 \times 10^{-5}$ K$^{-1}$. Find $\beta$ in (kC°)$^{-1}$. Note $1000$ C° $= 1$ kC°.

5. 1.3 L of water falls from 150.2 m ($g = 9.8$ m/s$^2$); find $\Delta T$ of water.

6. 3141 J transferred to 4.3 kg block, $c = 645$ J/(kg·C°). Find $\Delta T$.

7. 24,512 J raises 2.8 kg by 11 C°. Find $c$.

8. 5.6 kg chloroform at 13.5 C + 9.5 kg propylene glycol at 36.8 C; $c_{\text{prop}} = 2500$, $c_{\text{ch}} = 1050$ J/(kg·K). Find $T_f$.

9. 0.29 kg water at 17 C — how much energy must be removed to freeze? Use $L_f = 335{,}000$, $L_v = 2{,}260{,}000$, $c = 4190$.

10. 2.69 kg ice at −20 C → water at 16 C. Energy needed in kJ? Use $c_{\text{ice}} = 2100$, $c_{\text{water}} = 4190$, $L_f = 334 \times 10^3$.

11. 7.2 kg ice at 0 C + 11.5 kg water at 28.4 C. Find $T_f$. $L_f = 3.35 \times 10^5$, $c = 4190$. (Hint: partial or full melt?)

12. Window $1.6 \times 2$ m, thickness 13.9 mm, $k = 0.8$ W/(m·K). Outside −17 C, inside 18 C. Find $H$ in kW.

13. Ball radius 3.1 m, emissivity 0.63. Surface 92 C, room 28 C. Find net radiated $H$ in kW.
