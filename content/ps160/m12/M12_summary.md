## Density and Pressure

**Density** is mass per unit volume. For mass $m$ of homogeneous material in volume $V$:

$$\rho = \frac{m}{V} \quad (12.1)$$

**Specific gravity** is the ratio of a material's density to the density of water.

**Pressure** is normal force per unit area:

$$p = \frac{dF_\perp}{dA} \quad (12.2)$$

**Pascal's law**: pressure applied to an enclosed fluid is transmitted undiminished to every portion of the fluid.

- **Absolute pressure** is the total pressure in a fluid.
- **Gauge pressure** is the difference between absolute and atmospheric pressure.
- SI unit: pascal, $1~\mathrm{Pa} = 1~\mathrm{N/m^2}$.

## Pressures in a Fluid at Rest

In a static fluid of uniform density $\rho$ (incompressible):

$$p_2 - p_1 = -\rho g(y_2 - y_1) \quad (12.5)$$

If the surface pressure is $p_0$, then at depth $h$:

$$p = p_0 + \rho g h \quad (12.6)$$

> Figure: beaker of fluid of density $\rho$; point 1 at depth $h$ below surface, point 2 at surface with $p_2 = p_0$.

## Buoyancy

**Archimedes' principle**: when an object is immersed in a fluid, the fluid exerts an upward buoyant force on the object equal to the weight of the fluid that the object displaces.

$$F_B = \rho_\mathrm{fluid}\, V_\mathrm{displaced}\, g$$

> Figure: a fluid element replaced by a solid object of the same size/shape — same buoyant force.

## Fluid Flow

An **ideal fluid** is incompressible and has no viscosity (no internal friction).

- A **flow line** is the path of a fluid particle.
- A **streamline** is a curve tangent at each point to the velocity vector.
- A **flow tube** is bounded at its sides by flow lines.
- **Laminar flow**: layers slide smoothly past each other.
- **Turbulent flow**: disordered, rapidly changing pattern.

### Continuity equation (incompressible fluid)

$$A_1 v_1 = A_2 v_2 \quad (12.10)$$

### Volume flow rate

$$\frac{dV}{dt} = A v \quad (12.11)$$

### Bernoulli's equation (ideal fluid, steady flow)

$$p + \rho g y + \tfrac{1}{2}\rho v^2 = \text{constant} \quad (12.18)$$

This has the same value anywhere in a flow tube and relates properties of the flow at any two points.
