## Q3 — Energy: ice −17 °C → water 20 °C, $m=3.44$ kg

$Q = mc_{\text{ice}}(17) + mL_f + mc_w(20)$
$= 3.44(2100)(17) + 3.44(334000) + 3.44(4190)(20)$
$= 122808 + 1148960 + 288272 = \boxed{1560.04\ \text{kJ}}\ \checkmark$

## Q4 — Conduction through window

$A=6.3$ m², $d=0.0104$, $\Delta T=36$, $k=0.8$.
$H = kA\Delta T/d = 0.8(6.3)(36)/0.0104 = \boxed{17.446\ \text{kW}}\ \checkmark$

## Q5 — Venus surface atmospheric density

$\rho = PM/(RT)$ with $P = 91.39\cdot 101325 = 9.260\times 10^6$ Pa, $M = 0.04401$ kg/mol, $T = 724$ K.
$\rho = (9.260\times 10^6)(0.04401)/(8.314\cdot 724) = 407{,}532/6019.3 = \boxed{67.70\ \text{kg/m}^3}\ \checkmark$

## Q6 — Moles of C₂H₆

$M = 2(12.01) + 6(1.008) = 30.068$; $n = 70/30.068 = \boxed{2.328\ \text{mol}}\ \checkmark$

## Q7 — Translational KE

$K_{\text{tr}} = (3/2)Nk_BT = 1.5(231391)(1.381\times 10^{-23})(499) = 2.39\times 10^{-15}\ \text{J} = \boxed{2.39\ \text{fJ}}\ \checkmark$

## Q8 — Heat capacity of a solid (Dulong–Petit)

For a classical solid, molar heat capacity $\approx 3R$. $n = 13/61.9 = 0.21002$ mol.
$Q = n(3R)\Delta T = 0.21002(24.94)(17) = \boxed{89.05\ \text{J}}\ \checkmark$

## Q10 — First law

## Q11 — Diatomic isobaric, $\Delta U$

$\Delta U = (5/2)P\Delta V = 2.5(149)(0.8 - 2.9) = 2.5(149)(-2.1) = \boxed{-782.25\ \text{kJ}}\ \checkmark$

## Q12 — Diatomic adiabatic, $\Delta U$

$\gamma=1.4$. $P_f = 157(1/2.4)^{1.4} = 157(0.2937) = 46.11$ kPa.
$W_{\text{by}} = (P_iV_i - P_fV_f)/(\gamma-1) = (157 - 110.65)/0.4 = 115.86$ kJ.
$\Delta U = -W_{\text{by}} = \boxed{-115.86\ \text{kJ}}\ \checkmark$

## Q13 — Engine efficiency

$Q_H = Q_C + W = 2036 + 570 = 2606$; $e = W/Q_H = 570/2606 = \boxed{0.219}\ \checkmark$

## Q14 — Refrigerator

$|Q_H| = (\text{COP}+1)W$ ⇒ $W = 103/9 = 11.44$; $Q_C = 8(11.44) = \boxed{91.56\ \text{J}}\ \checkmark$

## Q15 — Carnot $T_C$

$T_H = 910.15$ K; $T_C = T_H(1-e) = 910.15(0.29) = \boxed{263.94\ \text{K}}\ \checkmark$

## Q16 — Universe entropy: water freezing into −18 °C room
