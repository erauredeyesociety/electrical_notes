# MQ34a

## Q1 — Flat mirror

(a) $-21$ cm, (b) $m=+1$, (c) virtual, (d) upright.

## Q2 — Concave mirror, $f=11.8$, $s=8.4$ (object inside focal length)

$1/s' = 1/11.8 - 1/8.4 = -0.0343 \Rightarrow s' = \boxed{-29.13\ \text{cm}}$ (virtual, upright)

## Q3 — Convex mirror, $|f|=12$, $s=20$

Convex ⇒ $f = -12$ cm.
$1/s' = -1/12 - 1/20 = -0.1333$ ⇒ $s' = \mathbf{-7.5\ \text{cm}}$ ✓
$m = -s'/s = 7.5/20 = \mathbf{+0.375}$ ✓
Image is **virtual** and **upright**.

## Q4 — Converging lens, $f=10$, $s=19.1$

$1/s' = 1/10 - 1/19.1 = 0.04764 \Rightarrow s' = \boxed{+20.99\ \text{cm}}$ (real, inverted)

## Q5 — Diverging lens, $f=-9$, $s=6$

$1/s' = -1/9 - 1/6 = -0.2778 \Rightarrow s' = \mathbf{-3.6\ \text{cm}}$ ✓
$m = -s'/s = 3.6/6 = \mathbf{+0.6}$ ✓
**Virtual** and **upright**.

# MQ34b

## Q1 — Lensmaker, then image position

$1/f = (1.9-1)(1/10 - 1/(-12)) = 0.9(0.1833) = 0.1650 \Rightarrow f = 6.061$ cm.
$1/s' = 1/6.061 - 1/25 = 0.1250 \Rightarrow s' = \boxed{+8.0\ \text{cm}}$

## Q2 — Coin inside crystal ball

Light goes from inside ($n_1=1.3$) to air ($n_2=1$); center of curvature is on the *incoming* side ⇒ $R = -39$ cm. Object 12 cm from surface ⇒ $s = +12$.
$$\dfrac{n_1}{s} + \dfrac{n_2}{s'} = \dfrac{n_2-n_1}{R}$$
$$\dfrac{1.3}{12} + \dfrac{1}{s'} = \dfrac{-0.3}{-39} = 0.007692$$
$$1/s' = 0.007692 - 0.10833 = -0.10064 \Rightarrow s' = \boxed{-9.93\ \text{cm}}$$
(Virtual image, on the same side as the object — the coin appears closer to the surface.)

## Q3 — Two converging lenses (origin-dependent)

Lens 1 ($f_1=7$): $1/s_1' = 1/7 - 1/12 = 0.0595 \Rightarrow s_1' = +16.8$ cm (real image, right of lens 1).
Object for lens 2: image of lens 1 is $18 - 16.8 = 1.2$ cm to the left of lens 2 ⇒ $s_2 = +1.2$.
$1/s_2' = 1/13 - 1/1.2 = -0.7564 \Rightarrow s_2' = -1.322$ cm (virtual, left of lens 2).

Final image position (origin at the tree): $x = 12 + 18 - 1.322 = \boxed{28.68\ \text{cm}}$. *Adjust if your figure puts $x=0$ elsewhere.*

## Q4 — Far point from lens power

$f = 1/P = 1/(-4.32) = -0.2315$ m $= -23.15$ cm.
For distant objects ($s\to\infty$), the lens forms an image at $s' = f$.
Far point distance: $\boxed{23.15\ \text{cm}}$ (in front of glasses).

## Q5 — Magnifier with $f=9.8$ cm

Standard simple magnifier: $M = 25/f = 25/9.8 = \boxed{2.55}$
(If your textbook uses $M = 1 + 25/f$: $M = 3.55$.)
