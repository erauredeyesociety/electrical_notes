# MQ19a

## Q1 — Isochoric, work by gas

$\Delta P$ at constant $V$ ⇒ $W_{\text{by}} = \boxed{0}$.

## Q2 — V-shape ramp (same figure as ME19 Q3)

Two trapezoidal legs $(V_1,p_2)\to(V_2,p_1)\to(V_3,p_2)$ sum to:
$W_{\text{by}} = \tfrac12(p_1+p_2)(V_3-V_1) = 0.5(509)(3.2) = +814.4$ kJ ⇒ $W_{\text{on}} = \boxed{-814.4\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q3 — PV_4 image, three pressures

⚠️ Image dependent. Most likely interpretation: two-leg ramp through three labeled volumes/pressures, e.g. $(V_1,p_1) \to (V_2,p_2) \to (V_3,p_3)$ (monotonic increase):
$W_{\text{by}} = \tfrac12(p_1+p_2)(V_2-V_1) + \tfrac12(p_2+p_3)(V_3-V_2)$
$= 0.5(356.4)(1.1) + 0.5(592.4)(1.3) = 196.0 + 385.1 = +581.1$ kJ ⇒ $W_{\text{on}} = \boxed{-581.1\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q4 — First law

$W_{\text{by}} = 0.35(602) = 210.7$ J; $\Delta U = Q - W_{\text{by}} = 602 - 210.7 = \boxed{391.3\ \text{J}}$

## Q5 — Monatomic isochoric, $\Delta U$

$\Delta U = (3/2)V\Delta P = 1.5(0.5)(166-98) = \boxed{51\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q6 — Constant V, work

$W = \boxed{0}$ kJ.

## Q7 — Monatomic isobaric, heat $Q$

$Q = (5/2)P\Delta V = 2.5(92)(3.8-1.6) = 2.5(92)(2.2) = \boxed{506\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q8 — Diatomic isobaric, heat $Q$

$Q = (7/2)P\Delta V = 3.5(182)(1.4-3.5) = 3.5(182)(-2.1) = \boxed{-1337.7\ \text{kJ}}$

# MQ19b

## Q1 — Isothermal compression, $W_{\text{by}}$

$W_{\text{by}} = P_iV_i\ln(V_f/V_i) = 60(2.7)\ln(1/2.7) = 162(-0.9933) = \boxed{-160.91\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q2 — Isothermal expansion, $W_{\text{on}}$

$W_{\text{by}} = 136(1)\ln(2) = 94.27$ kJ; $W_{\text{on}} = \boxed{-94.27\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q3 — Monatomic adiabatic compression, $P_f$

$\gamma = 5/3$; $P_f = P_i(V_i/V_f)^\gamma = 196(3/1.6)^{5/3} = 196(2.8511) = \boxed{558.8\ \text{kPa}}$

## Q4 — Diatomic adiabatic expansion: $P_f$ and $\Delta U$

$\gamma=1.4$; $P_f = 150(1/2)^{1.4} = 150(0.3789) = 56.83$ kPa ⇒ **56.8 kPa** ✓
$W_{\text{by}} = (P_iV_i - P_fV_f)/(\gamma-1) = (150 - 113.65)/0.4 = 90.86$ kJ
$\Delta U = -W_{\text{by}} = \boxed{-90.86\ \text{kJ}}$ ⇒ **−90.8 kJ** ✓

## Q5 — Monatomic adiabatic expansion, $W_{\text{by}}$

$P_f = 247(1/2.2)^{5/3} = 247(0.2687) = 66.37$ kPa.
$W_{\text{by}} = (247 - 66.37(2.2))/(2/3) = (247 - 146.01)/0.6667 = \boxed{151.5\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q6 — Straight ramp on PV

$W_{\text{by}} = \tfrac12(260+487)(4-3) = \boxed{373.5\ \text{kJ}}$
