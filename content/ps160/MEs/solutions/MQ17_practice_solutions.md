# MQ17a

## Q1 — 61 °C → K

$T_K = 61 + 273.15 = \boxed{334.15\ \text{K}}$

## Q2 — $\Delta T$ for shrinkage

$\alpha=4\times 10^{-5}$, $L_0=2.4$, $\Delta L=-0.0047$.
$\Delta T = \Delta L/(\alpha L_0) = -0.0047/(9.6\times 10^{-5}) = \boxed{-48.96\ \text{°C}}$

## Q3 — Linear coefficient

$\alpha = \Delta L/(L_0\Delta T) = 0.018/(11.5\cdot 57) = 2.747\times 10^{-5}$ ⇒ $\boxed{2.747\times 10^{-5}\ (\text{°C})^{-1}}$

## Q4 — Volume increase

$V_0 = 5.376\ \text{m}^3$; $\beta = 3\alpha = 5.7\times 10^{-5}$.
$\Delta V = \beta V_0\,\Delta T = 5.7\times 10^{-5}\cdot 5.376\cdot 5.9 = 1.808\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3 = \boxed{1808\ \text{cm}^3}$

## Q5 — $\Delta T$ from $Q$

$\Delta T = 4498/(1.5\cdot 637) = 4498/955.5 = \boxed{4.708\ \text{°C}}$

## Q6 — Iron + water equilibrium

$\sum m c (T - T_0) = 0$:
$6.5(444)(T - 89) + 0.3(4190)(T - 24) = 0$
$T = (6.5\cdot 444\cdot 89 + 0.3\cdot 4190\cdot 24)/(6.5\cdot 444 + 0.3\cdot 4190) = 287{,}022/4143 = \boxed{69.27\ \text{°C}}$

# MQ17b

## Q1 — Mass of hot water to add

$m_1 c(T_f - T_1) + m_2 c(T_f - T_2) = 0$
$0.72(35-10) = m_2(90-35) \Rightarrow m_2 = 18/55 = \boxed{0.3273\ \text{kg}}$

## Q2 — Energy to melt block

$Q = mL_f = 8(160829) = 1{,}286{,}632\ \text{J} = \boxed{1286.6\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q3 — Ice + water equilibrium

Heat avail (water 27 → 0): $11.5(4190)(27) = 1.301\times 10^6$ J.
Heat to melt all ice: $4.5(335000) = 1.5075\times 10^6$ J.
Less available than needed ⇒ partial melt ⇒ $\boxed{T_{\text{eq}} = 0\ \text{°C}}$

## Q4 — Conduction through window

$A = 3\ \text{m}^2$, $d=0.0134$, $\Delta T=24$, $k=0.8$.
$H = kA\Delta T/d = 0.8(3)(24)/0.0134 = \boxed{4.299\ \text{kW}}$

## Q5 — Surface temperature from radiation

$H = \varepsilon\sigma A(T_s^4 - T_{\text{env}}^4)$ with $T_{\text{env}} = 296.15$ K, $T_{\text{env}}^4 = 7.692\times 10^9$.
$T_s^4 = 7.692\times 10^9 + 2685/(0.57\cdot 5.67\times 10^{-8}\cdot 0.68) = 7.692\times 10^9 + 1.222\times 10^{11} = 1.299\times 10^{11}$
$T_s = (1.299\times 10^{11})^{1/4} = 600.0$ K $= \boxed{326.85\ \text{°C}}$
