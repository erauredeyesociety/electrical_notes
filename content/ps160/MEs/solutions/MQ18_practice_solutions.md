# MQ18a

## Q1 — V at STP for $n=1.8$

$V = nRT/P = 1.8(8.314)(273)/101325 = 0.04032\ \text{m}^3 = \boxed{40.32\ \text{L}}$

## Q2 — Pressure of Krypton in tank

$M_{\text{Kr}} = 0.0838$ kg/mol; $n = 0.002777/0.0838 = 0.03314$ mol; $T=303.15$ K; $V = 7\times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^3$.
$P = nRT/V = (0.03314)(8.314)(303.15)/0.007 = 83.55/0.007 = \boxed{11.94\ \text{kPa}}$

## Q3 — Rising bubble (isothermal)

$P_i = P_{\text{surface}} + \rho g h = 94043 + 1000(9.8)(8) = 172{,}443$ Pa.
Boyle: $V_f = V_i (P_i/P_{\text{surface}}) = 158(172443/94043) = \boxed{289.7\ \text{mm}^3}$

## Q4 — Moles of C₂H₄

$M = 2(12) + 4(1) = 28$ g/mol; $n = 81/28 = \boxed{2.893\ \text{mol}}$

## Q5 — Mass of one C₂H₃OH molecule

$M = 2(12.011) + 4(1.008) + 16.00 = 44.05$ g/mol $= 0.04405$ kg/mol.
$m_1 = M/N_A = 0.04405/6.022\times 10^{23} = 7.316\times 10^{-26}$ kg
$\Rightarrow \boxed{73.16\times 10^{-27}\ \text{kg}}$

# MQ18b

## Q1 — T from per-atom translational KE

$\langle KE\rangle = \tfrac{3}{2}k_B T \Rightarrow T = 2\langle KE\rangle/(3k_B) = 2(5.5\times 10^{-22})/(3\cdot 1.381\times 10^{-23})$
$T = 1.1\times 10^{-21}/4.143\times 10^{-23} = \boxed{26.55\ \text{K}}$

## Q2 — Total $K_{\text{tr}}$ for given $N$, $T$

$K_{\text{tr}} = \tfrac{3}{2}Nk_BT = 1.5(600796)(1.381\times 10^{-23})(552) = 6.87\times 10^{-15}\ \text{J} = \boxed{6.87\ \text{fJ}}$

(Volume is a distractor — not needed for translational KE.)

## Q3 — Most probable speed of O₂ at 97.7 °C

$T = 370.85$ K, $M = 0.032$ kg/mol.
$v_p = \sqrt{2RT/M} = \sqrt{2(8.314)(370.85)/0.032} = \sqrt{192{,}690} = \boxed{438.97\ \text{m/s}}$

## Q4 — T from $v_{\text{rms}}$

$T = M v_{\text{rms}}^2/(3R) = (0.01)(628^2)/(3\cdot 8.314) = 3943.84/24.942 = \boxed{158.13\ \text{K}}$

## Q5 — $\Delta U$ for $f=4$

$\Delta U = (f/2)nR\Delta T = 2(1.5)(8.314)(54) = 1346.9\ \text{J} = \boxed{1.347\ \text{kJ}}$

## Q6 — Internal energy of helium

He is monatomic, $f=3$, $T=298.15$ K.
$U = \tfrac{3}{2}nRT = 1.5(6.6)(8.314)(298.15) = \boxed{24.54\ \text{kJ}}$
