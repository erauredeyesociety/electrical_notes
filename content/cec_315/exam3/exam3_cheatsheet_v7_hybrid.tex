\textbf{Laplace.} $X(s)=\!\int x(t)e^{-st}dt$, $s=\sigma+j\omega$. Unilateral: lower limit $0^-$. FT exists iff $j\omega$-axis $\subset$ ROC.
\textbf{Z.} $X(z)=\!\sum x[n]z^{-n}$, $z=re^{j\Omega}$. Unilateral: $n\ge 0$. DTFT exists iff unit circle $\subset$ ROC.
\textbf{ROC rules (both).} Never contain poles. Right-sided: $\sigma>\sigma_{\max}$ / $|z|>r_{\max}$. Left: reverse. Two-sided: strip / annulus. Finite-duration: entire $s$/$z$ plane (except $0,\infty$). \textbf{Causal+stable} $\Leftrightarrow$ LHP poles (CT) / $|p|<1$ (DT).
\textbf{PFE --- Distinct:} $\sum A_k/(s-p_k)$ with cover-up $A_k=(s-p_k)X|_{p_k}$.
\textbf{Repeated} order $r$ at $p$: $\sum B_k/(s-p)^k$ with $B_k=\tfrac{1}{(r-k)!}\tfrac{d^{r-k}}{ds^{r-k}}[(s-p)^r X]|_p$.
\textbf{Complex conj.} $p=-a\pm j\omega_d$: complete square $(s+a)^2+\omega_d^2$, use $\cos/\sin$ pairs.
\textbf{Z:} always in $z^{-1}$ (never $z$); ROC selects right/left per pole.
\textbf{Improper rationals.} Long-divide first. Laplace: const remainder $\to\delta(t)$; higher polynomial $\to\delta^{(k)}(t)$; PFE strictly-proper. Z: polynomial in $z^{-1}$ $\to$ finite-duration samples $\delta[n{-}k]$.

\textbf{CT (ODE).} $y^{(k)}\!\to\! s^k Y - \sum_{j=0}^{k-1}\! s^{k-1-j} y^{(j)}(0^-)$. Solve $Y=Y_{\text{ZS}}+Y_{\text{ZI}}$; PFE; invert. $H(s)=Y_{\text{ZS}}/X$.
\textbf{DT (diff.\ eq.).} $y[n{-}k]\to z^{-k}Y + \sum_{m=1}^k z^{-(k-m)}y[-m]$. Solve; PFE in $z^{-1}$; invert. $H(z)=Y_{\text{ZS}}/X$.
Total = ZSR (ICs$=0$, input only) + ZIR (input$=0$, ICs only).
\textbf{Unilateral Laplace IVP.} Transform ICs (\textbf{minus}) $\to$ solve $Y$ $\to$ split ZSR+ZIR $\to$ PFE $\to$ invert $\to$ verify $y(0^+)$, $y(\infty)$.
\textbf{Unilateral Z diff eqn.} Transform ICs (\textbf{plus}) $\to$ solve $Y$ $\to$ PFE in $z^{-1}$ $\to$ invert $\to$ check $y[0], y[1]$ from diff eqn.
\textbf{Sign trap.} Unil.\ L: $\mathbf{-}y(0^-)$. Unil.\ Z: $\mathbf{+}y[-1]$. Opposite signs.
\textbf{Theorem (sampling).} Bandlimited to $\omega_M$ $\Rightarrow$ perfect recovery iff $\omega_s>2\omega_M$ (\emph{strict}). $\omega_s=2\omega_M$ can fail (sine at zero crossings).

\textbf{Reconstruction.} Pass $X_p$ through LPF with passband gain $T$, cutoff in $(\omega_M,\omega_s{-}\omega_M)$; equivalent time-domain: sinc interpolation $x_r=\sum x(nT)\sinc(\tfrac{t-nT}{T})$. ZOH is NOT exact --- sinc-shaped droop.
\textbf{Aliasing is irreversible.} Use anti-aliasing LPF \emph{before} the sampler, never after. For a pure tone $f_0>f_s/2$: aliased frequency is $|f_s-f_0|$; sign flip means reversed apparent direction (helicopter blade).
\textbf{Nyquist rate transforms.} Time-shift $x(t-t_0)$ keeps rate. Time-scale $x(at)$ scales rate by $|a|$.
\textbf{Bandwidth of products.} $x_1 x_2$ has BW $\le \omega_{M,1}+\omega_{M,2}$; squaring doubles BW.
\textbf{Feedback.} Closed-loop poles = roots of $1+GH=0$. Cascade multiplies, parallel adds, nested loops reduce innermost first.
\textbf{Nyquist criterion.} $N=Z-P$: $N$ CW encirclements of $-1$, $P$ open-loop RHP poles, $Z$ closed-loop RHP poles. Need $Z=0$. Open-loop stable ($P=0$): closed-loop stable iff Nyquist plot does \emph{not} encircle $-1$ (or $-1/K$).
\textbf{Gain/phase margins.} $\omega_{gc}$ is where $|L|=1$; PM is phase lead there. $\omega_{pc}$ is where $\angle L=-180^\circ$; GM is magnitude-below-0-dB there. Both $>0 \Rightarrow$ stable. Delay tolerance $\tau_{\max}=\mathrm{PM}_\mathrm{rad}/\omega_{gc}$.
\textbf{Routh--Hurwitz.} Sign changes in first column $=$ \# RHP roots. Necessary: all $a_i>0$, none missing.
\textbf{First-order plants.} GM $=\infty$.

\textbf{Top traps.}
\textbf{Always state ROC.} Same $X$, different ROC = different signal.
Unil.\ L: $\mathbf{-}y(0^-)$. Unil.\ Z: $\mathbf{+}y[-1]$. Opposite signs.
Repeated pole order $n$ needs $n$ PFE terms.
DT: $|a|<1$, not $a<1$. Pole at $z=-0.9$ IS stable.
FVT invalid if unstable or marginal (poles on $j\omega$ or unit circle).
$\omega_s>2\omega_M$ strict; aliasing irreversible; AAF \emph{before} sampler.
GM read from $|L|$ at $-180^\circ$; PM from $\angle L$ at 0 dB. Don't swap.
Poles of $\tfrac{1}{1-az^{-1}}$ at $z=a$, not $1/a$.
PFE in Z: use $z^{-1}$, never $z$.
RHP \emph{zero} $\ne$ instability (only poles).
Neg fdbk $\to 1+GH$; pos $\to 1-GH$.
Negative feedback does \emph{not} always stabilize (too much gain $\to$ unstable).
