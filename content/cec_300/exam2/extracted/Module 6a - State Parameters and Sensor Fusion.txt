As discussed earlier in the course, there are a 
variety of sensors whose inputs provide a 
partial picture at the aircraft’s state.
o Position – GPS, Accelerometer, Air data 
system
o Velocity – GPS, Air Data System, 
accelerometer
o Acceleration – Accelerometer
o Roll – Accelerometer, thermopile, multiple 
GPS receivers
o Yaw – magnetometer and/or gyroscope
o Pitch – Altimeter and accelerometer
o Encoders – measure actual position of 
rudder, aileron, flaps, etc.
•
For a control system to work accurately, an 
approach is needed to combine the data from 
the various data sources to improve the 
estimated state of the aircraft (i.e. sensor 
fusion)
•
Motivation for multi-sensor fusion: 
o Redundancy
o State estimation error reduction
o Different update rates

operation under R/C control.  
•

position when hands-off.  
•
Trim mechanisms can be installed on control surfaces to assist pilot in 
manually dialing in trim for example, trim tabs on elevators allow the 
pilot to adjust the hands-off pitch of the aircraft.
