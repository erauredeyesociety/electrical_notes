Sensors
• Sun Sensors
• Determines the Sun relative position
• Star Trackers
• Estimate position based on known landmarks 
(Stars) within the imager’s field of view
• Inertial Measurement Units or Gyroscopes
• Indicates the angular rates about each axes.
• Earth Horizon Sensor – for missions where the 
satellite must point toward Earth, indicates the 
Earth’s horizon relative to the spacecraft.
Actuators
• Reaction Wheels
• controls rotational rate about an axes
• Control Moment Gyroscopes – enables rapid 
maneuvers of large spacecraft
• Magnetorquers
• Provides torque about an axes commonly used for 
small satellite ADCS and desaturation of reaction 
wheels.
• Reaction Control Thrusters
• Small thruster which uses gas expellant for 
attitude control about each axis.
• Applications include detumbling, reaction wheel 
desaturation, and large maneuvers.

This technique leverages the spin of the spacecraft at

Example: Earth’s H-vector points out of its north poll 
because of a constant angular rotation.
