Module 1 Overview and Objectives

Introduction to Probability

 Overview

Probability is the study of chance and deals with predicting the likelihood of future events. Probability is primarily a theoretical branch of mathematics that studies the consequences of mathematical decisions. For example, think about how many decisions you personally make every day. Can you estimate how many decisions a typical person makes every day? Recent studies suggest 35,000 decisions are made per day, and with the rapid development in technology yielding even more choices, this number is expected to climb rapidly. Accordingly, probability enables us to determine the possible consequences of a given ideal world and helps us understand what influences our 35,000 daily decisions.

Throughout this module, think about how you employ the concept of probability every day.

 Module Objectives

Upon successful completion of this module, you will be able to:

  1. Describe sample spaces and events for random experiments with graphs, tables, lists, or tree diagrams. (LO1)
  2. Interpret probabilities and outcomes in the calculation of probabilities of events in discrete sample spaces. (LO3)
  3. Use permutations and combinations to count the number of outcomes in both an event and the sample space. (LO1)
  4. Calculate the probabilities of joint events such as unions and intersections from the probabilities of individual events. (LO3)
  5. Interpret the calculated conditional probabilities of events. (LO3)
  6. Determine the independence of events and use independence to calculate probabilities. (LO3)
  7. Use Bayes' theorem to calculate conditional probabilities. (LO2)
  8. Interpret computed statistics: sample mean, sample variance, sample standard deviation, sample median, and sample range. (LO7, LO12)
  9. Interpret visual data displays - including the stem-and-leaf display, the histogram, scatterplots, and the box plot - constructed from given data. (LO6)
  10. Explain the concept of random sampling. (LO1)
  11. Analyze data to form statistically sound conclusions. (LO1-13)
  12. Evaluate course learning outcomes. (LO1-13)
